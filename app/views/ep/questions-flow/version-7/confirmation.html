When you contact the pharmacy

You need to tell them you were referred by 111.

Tell the pharmacist what medicine you need and when the next dose is due.

The pharmacist will then tell you:

  • if they have it in stock
  • if it is safe to give you the medicine
  • what to do if they cannot give you the medicine

Getting the medicine sent to you

If the pharmacist can supply the medicine they will arrange to deliver it to you.

The medicine should turn up in time for you to take your next dose. If it does not, contact the pharmacy again.

You will not have to pay for the delivery.

Contacting them before you go

You need to tell them you were referred by NHS 111.

You can call the pharmacy before you go to tell them what medicine you need.

The pharmacist will then tell you:

  • if they have it in stock
  • if it is safe to give you the medicine
  • what to do if they cannot give you the medicine

If you do not call them before you go, the pharmacist will check this information when you arrive.

If the pharmacy has no stock

If the medicine is not in stock, the pharmacist will contact other local pharmacies to find it for you.

It is the pharmacist's responsibility to help you get the medicine you need.

What happens when you get there

If you did not contact them before arriving, the pharmacist will:

  • check your records to see what you are regularly prescribed
  • ask you some questions to get any information they need

You might need to show proof of identity when you get there, such as photo ID or a credit or debit card.
